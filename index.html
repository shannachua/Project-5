STUDIO GHIBLI

Spirited Away

2001

Directed by Hayao Miyazaki, Spirited Away is a critically acclaimed Studio Ghibli film about a young girl named Chihiro who becomes trapped in a mysterious spirit world. To save her parents, who have been turned into pigs, Chihiro works in a bathhouse for spirits, facing magical challenges and learning about courage, kindness, and self-discovery. Known for its stunning animation and emotional depth, it won an Academy Award for Best Animated Feature.

Howl's Moving Castle

2004

Howl's Moving Castle, directed by Hayao Miyazaki, is a fantastical tale about Sophie, a young woman cursed by a witch to become an old lady. She seeks refuge in the magical moving castle of the mysterious wizard Howl, embarking on a journey filled with magic, love, and self-empowerment. The film is praised for its imaginative visuals, complex characters, and anti-war themes.

Kiki's Delivery Service

1989

This heartwarming coming-of-age film, directed by Hayao Miyazaki, follows Kiki, a young witch who moves to a seaside town to begin her independent life. With her talking cat Jiji, she starts a delivery service using her broomstick but struggles with self-doubt and finding her place in the world. The film is celebrated for its charming animation, relatable themes, and uplifting message about perseverance and self-belief.
